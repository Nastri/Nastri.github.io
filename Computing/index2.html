  • Introduction

    For my project in Term 3 I competed in the NCSS Intermediate Challenge for 2019. The NCSS challenge is a coding competition where students from around the world solve coding problems and is designed for middle school students and above. I had done the challenge previously, so I had some experience in the field.

    Objectives

    • Learn new coding skills
    • Improve my analytical and computational thinking
    • Solve problems based on real world problems

    Results


    • I learnt new solutions to problems
    • I improved my analysing skills
    • I achieved a High Distinction

    Process

    After setting up my account, and logging on to Grok Learning I was ready for my project which was the NCSS Challenge for 2019. Many of the problems in the challenge were thought provoking and made you think about the solution. As the weeks went on the challenge got progressively harder as time went on. By the time of the 5th week the challenges were very challenging. The challenges varied per week and required different sets of skills.

    Discussion

    There were several challenging problems to solve in the NCSS Challenge. One type of problems that I found hard was the ones where you access data from a list or a file and printing it out with a name and a numerical value. The cipher problems were also quite challenging at first but once I got my head around it, I found it easier. One thing that I could have done better was finishing one of the problems that I couldn’t complete in week 3 as I found that particular problem extremely hard to get my head around. When the problems were unclear or confusing I was able to get help from Mr Morffew or one of the online tutors available during the challenge. I did a good job with completing all the problems except 1, and I came out with a High Distinction in the end. I did well with solving the problems and keeping up with the pace of the competition.
